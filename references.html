A curated list of about 45 academic papers, books, and policy documents on Bibliobattle, most of which are originally published in Japanese. Each entry provides an English summary, the original Japanese title, and a direct link to the source whenever available.

A. Works by the Inventor (Tadahiro Taniguchi)

A1 Bibliobattle: Construction of an Interface that Generates Connections through Book Reviews ビブリオバトル：書評で繋がりを生成するインタフェースの構築
Taniguchi, T., Kawakami, H., Katai, O. — Proceedings of the Human Interface Society, 2009
The first academic paper on Bibliobattle. The authors theoretically position Bibliobattle as an interface that generates connections "between people" and "between people and books" via book reviews. They argue that Bibliobattle's deliberately imposed constraints — no slides, a strict five-minute limit — paradoxically draw out the speakers' enthusiasm and individuality that conventional reading clubs fail to elicit. Influenced by the Web 2.0 era's notion of collective intelligence, the paper envisions a fusion of local face-to-face book reviews and global internet dissemination.
[Original PDF (Japanese)]
A2 Bibliobattle: An Informal Social Interaction Design Mediated by Book Reviews 書評により媒介される社会的相互作用場の設計：ビブリオバトル
Taniguchi, T., Kawakami, H., Katai, O. — Transactions of the Human Interface Society, Vol. 12, No. 4, pp. 427–437, 2010
The peer-reviewed extension of A1. This paper formalizes Bibliobattle as a "design of a social interaction field mediated by book reviews," explicitly distinguishing the locality of face-to-face community from the globality of online information sharing. The authors articulate Bibliobattle's four core functions: (i) book discovery, (ii) information sharing, (iii) speech-skill development, and (iv) community formation, which together support the dynamism of the game. This formulation has served as the foundational framework for subsequent educational research.
[J-STAGE]
A3 Designing Presentation Time of Bibliobattle
Taniguchi, T., et al. — c. 2016
An empirical investigation of the rationale behind Bibliobattle's five-minute presentation rule. Through experiments comparing 1-, 3-, 5-, 7-, and 10-minute conditions, the authors find that presentations of 3, 5, and 7 minutes do not differ significantly in informational quality, but that the 1-minute and 10-minute conditions diverge significantly. The study provides data-grounded justification for the canonical five-minute rule.
A4 A Book Review Game in Japan — Bibliobattle and Its Potential as a Teaching Tool
Taniguchi, T. — c. 2015 (ProQuest)
An English-language introduction of Bibliobattle aimed at international library and education professionals. Surveys the spread from Kyoto University's invention in 2007 to its adoption by libraries, bookstores, and other cultural organizations, with a particular focus on its potential as a teaching tool.
A5 Analyzing Listeners' Empathy by Their Nonverbal Behaviors in Bibliobattle
Matsumura, K., Taniguchi, T., et al. — 2017
A study of nonverbal behaviours (nodding, laughter, posture changes) of audience members during Bibliobattle sessions. The authors observe co-occurrence of nonverbal cues among listeners — laughter co-occurrence indicates a presentation's liveliness, nodding co-occurrence suggests agreement — yet these frequencies do not directly correlate with the Champion Book vote outcomes, an unexpected finding that opens new questions about the determinants of voting behaviour.
A-B1 Bibliobattle: A Book Review Game for Knowing Books and Knowing People ビブリオバトル 本を知り人を知る書評ゲーム
Taniguchi, T. — Bungeishunju (Bunshun Shinsho), 2013
The inventor's first general-audience book on Bibliobattle. Written in the first person, it traces the origin, design rationale, and dissemination of the game, explaining the underlying concept of "communication-field design" in language accessible to non-specialists. The book's publication itself marked a turning point in Bibliobattle's social recognition.
A-B2 Introduction to Bibliobattle: Knowing People through Books, Knowing Books through People ビブリオバトル入門 — 本を通して人を知る・人を通して本を知る
Bibliobattle Promotion Committee, Yoshino, H., Suto, H., Otani, Y., Taniguchi, T. — Bunken Shuppan, 2013
The official guidebook, co-authored by the Bibliobattle Promotion Committee with the inventor. Provides comprehensive coverage of practical implementation, variants (including Mini-Bibliobattle), and operational know-how, serving as a manual for adoption in schools, libraries, and corporate training.
A-M1 Bibliobattle in the Age of Generative AI: Knowing What It Means to Be Human through Books 生成AI時代のビブリオバトル──本を通して、人「であること」を知る
Taniguchi, T. — Mita Hyoron Online, 2023
An essay arguing that as generative AI begins to summarize, recommend, and even author book reviews, Bibliobattle's value lies precisely in foregrounding the irreplaceable presence of a human speaker talking about a book in their own words.

Journal of Japan Society of Information and Knowledge, Vol. 27, No. 2, 2017

C. Educational Practice — Higher Education

C3 Factors Promoting Interaction in Mini-Bibliobattle ミニ・ビブリオバトルにおいて相互作用が促進される要因に関する考察
Jono — Nagoya Gakuin University Review: Language and Culture, Vol. 30, No. 1
★ Key finding: Through a questionnaire-based statistical analysis of Mini-Bibliobattle sessions, the author identifies "structuring" — the explicit prior specification of how to read, speak, and listen — as significantly correlated with smooth interaction and discussion. Surprisingly, the consciousness of "externalization" (verbalizing one's thoughts) does not by itself activate interaction. This result supports the design philosophy that constraints, rather than freedom, generate deeper communication.
[NGU Repository]
C6 Battling With Books: The Gamification of an EFL Extensive Reading Class
Freiermuth, M. R., & Ito, M. — Simulation & Gaming (SAGE), 2022
A peer-reviewed English-language study with 55 Japanese female undergraduates. Three Bibliobattle sessions integrated into an EFL extensive-reading course significantly raised reading motivation, especially for lower-proficiency learners who had previously been disengaged with the activity. The authors recommend a maximum of two Bibliobattle sessions per semester due to preparation overhead. The most prominent international peer-reviewed evidence to date on the educational efficacy of Bibliobattle.
[SAGE]
C8 Practice and Impact of Bibliobattle on Students at a Junior College 短期大学におけるビブリオバトルの実践と学生への影響
Tsuchiya — Akita Junior College Bulletin, Vol. 41, No. 2, 2024
★ Key data: While roughly 42% of students are willing to attend Bibliobattle as audience, only about 10% want to participate as battlers (presenters). The author argues that the psychological burden of speaking unrehearsed for five minutes and being publicly evaluated by vote constitutes a significant barrier to entry, calling for staged, psychologically safe approaches to introduction.
[Akita JC Repository]
C4 / C5 / C7 / C9 / C10
Other higher-education studies include: Jono (CELES Vol. 48) on the role of structuring in extensive reading; a narrative analysis of Mini-Bibliobattle (CELES Vol. 49); a theoretical/practical treatment of Mini-Bibliobattle as output activity (TPLS Vol. 8); an online Bibliobattle implementation in an intermediate Japanese-as-a-second-language class (HUSCAP); and continued practice in early-childhood educator training (Bunkyo University Bulletin).

D. Educational Practice — Secondary Education

D1 On the Effects of Introducing Bibliobattle in Japanese Expression Classes 「国語表現」におけるビブリオバトル導入の効果について
Tatsumoto, Natsuko — Osaka University of Arts (Research Note)
A high-school practice study aligned with the official "Kokugo Hyogen" (Japanese Expression) curriculum standards. Through the cognitively demanding task of speaking for five minutes without notes, students actively learned the knowledge required for oral expression and audience-aware explanation. Post-activity self-reports document gains in both expressive ability and reading interest, demonstrating that Bibliobattle simultaneously advances reading promotion and expression skill development.
[PDF]
D2 The Effectiveness of Bidirectional Communication in Bibliobattle ビブリオバトルにおける双方向コミュニケーションの有効性
Katsuyama, Hiroko — Tokoha University Bulletin, Vol. 51, No. 1, 2020
★ Key data: A quantitative comparison of attitudes between Champion-Book presenters and non-Champion presenters. Champion-Book presenters report higher enjoyment (71.9% vs 47.2%), while non-Champion presenters become more active during the discussion period (37.5% vs 27.1%). The vote outcome thus does not collapse into mere ranking; instead it functions as a divergent route of participation, securing the receptivity of the community rather than rupturing it.
[Tokoha Repository]
D3 / D4
Other secondary-education studies include: a remote-class implementation report (Kosei Gakuen High School) and an ICT-augmented Bibliobattle initiative (Panasonic Education Foundation, 2018).

E. Library and Information Science / Reading Promotion

E1 Bibliobattle and Children's Reading Promotion Plans ビブリオバトルと子ども読書活動推進計画
Okano, Hiroyuki — Toshokankai (The Library World), Vol. 73, No. 5, p. 438–
★ Key data: A comprehensive survey of how Japan's prefectural and municipal "Children's Reading Promotion Plans" mention Bibliobattle. As of 2019, 87.2% of prefectures and 17.8% of municipalities had explicit references to Bibliobattle, demonstrating its institutional integration into national reading promotion policy.
[J-STAGE]
E2 Multi-Directional Development Trends of Bibliobattle 新しい本の楽しみ方「ビブリオバトル」の多方面への展開動向
Yoshino, Hidetomo — Current Awareness (National Diet Library), CA1830
Published by the National Diet Library, this professional review surveys Bibliobattle's diverse expansion across libraries, education, media, and community contexts, positioning it as a new method of reading promotion from a library and information science perspective.
[NDL]
E7 Research Trends on Bibliobattle in Japan 日本におけるビブリオバトルに関する研究動向
Komagome — Seisen Jogakuin College Bulletin, 2023
The most direct prior survey to ours. As of 2022, Bibliobattle had been held at 303 universities (37.7%) and 351 public libraries (10.6%), with confirmed events in all 47 prefectures. Argues that Bibliobattle has moved beyond a transient boom into an institutionalized form of reading promotion, with formal inclusion in the Glossary of Library and Information Science.
[Seisen Repository]
E3 – E10
Other library-science items include: practice reports in Joho no Kagaku to Gijutsu (Vol. 63, 66, 68), a community-academia collaboration study (Tottori University of Environmental Studies, Vol. 12, p. 53–64), and a JUAA case study on student development through library–community collaboration.

F. Japanese Language Education

F1 An Attempt to Introduce the Book Review Game "Bibliobattle" 書評ゲーム「ビブリオバトル」導入の試み
Journal of Japanese Language Teaching, Vol. 155, p. 175
A practice report on introducing Bibliobattle as public-speaking training in a Japanese-as-a-second-language course. Indicates that Bibliobattle effectively improves L2 public-speaking skills.
[J-STAGE]

G. International Expansion

G1 Bibliobattle as a Promotion Strategy: A Case Study at the Library of the Ministry of Social Affairs of the Republic of Indonesia
Panuntun et al. — Berkala Ilmu Perpustakaan dan Informasi, UGM, 2019
A case study of Bibliobattle adopted as a library promotion strategy at the Indonesian Ministry of Social Affairs Library. Reports that Bibliobattle contributed not only to library awareness but also to increased use of the collection, exemplifying the cross-cultural transferability of the format.
[Journal UGM]

H. Policy Documents

H1 Third Basic Plan for the Promotion of Children's Reading Activities 第三次子どもの読書活動の推進に関する基本的な計画
Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT), 2013
The official policy document in which Bibliobattle was first explicitly named as a recommended reading-promotion activity, accelerating its adoption across schools nationwide.
H2 Library Practice Case Collection: Toward Proactive, Interactive, and Deep Learning
MEXT
A government-issued case collection featuring multiple Bibliobattle implementations in school libraries, publicly recognizing the alignment between Bibliobattle and the curriculum's emphasis on "proactive, interactive, and deep learning."
[MEXT]

I. Psychology / Metacognition

I2 Sharing the Charms of Books: Building Effective Self-Evaluation by Exchanging Impressions and Suggestions 本の魅力を紹介し合おう〜感想や助言を伝え合うことで効果的な自己評価へとつなげる
Sanseido — Practice Report (Junior High School Japanese Language)
A practice report from a major Japanese textbook publisher. Documents how students develop metacognitive awareness of their own communication style by recommending books to one another and exchanging feedback. The fact that Bibliobattle is featured as a textbook-aligned practice indicates the depth of its institutional adoption.
[Sanseido]
